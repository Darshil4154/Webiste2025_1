Research Paper

Loan Distribution and Prediction System using ML

In this research project, I explored the potential of machine learning to improve risk assessment and loan default prediction within the financial sector. Using a real-world dataset from the Lending Club (2007–2015), we processed over 850,000 loan records and applied advanced data cleaning, feature engineering, and model evaluation techniques to build a predictive system. The core goal was to identify patterns and risk factors that contribute to loan default, enabling lenders to make informed and data-driven decisions. Various algorithms—such as Logistic Regression and Random Forest—were evaluated, with logistic models providing strong overall accuracy, though further work is needed to improve precision for defaulters due to class imbalance in the dataset.

This research not only showcases the practical application of data science in finance but also offers insights for institutions aiming to reduce loan-related risks. Key recommendations include closer scrutiny of high-risk categories such as small business loans, self-employed applicants, and those with lower annual incomes.

📌 Published in: International Journal of All Research Education and Scientific Methods (IJARESM), Vol. 12, Issue 1, Jan 2024
